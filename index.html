MS Dhoni leads India to 2nd World Cup title after 28 years

Mumbai :

India beat Sri Lanka by 6 Wickets to lift their 2nd World Cup Trophy.

April 2, 2011 Today, MS Dhoni and his men gave a billion Indian Cricket fans plenty of joy as India trumped Sri Lanka in the World Cup final at the packed Wankhede Stadium in Mumbai to end their 28-year-old title drought. It is a triumph that will be remembered for ages as India had waited for 27 years, 9 months and 7 days to add 2nd World Cup to their kitty.

The image of skipper MS Dhoni smashing Sri Lanka's Nuwan Kulasekara for a six to seal the victory is etched in the heart of every Indian. With that decisive six that has defined Dhoni's career ever since he lead India to their first World Cup triumph since 1983 and sent the entitre India in an absolute frenzy.

MS Dhoni, promoting himself up the order, joined Gambhir and they both added 109 runs, an Indian record in a World Cup final.
Fittingly the team won it for SACHIN says Captain. Lifting sachin on my shoulder made the night more memorable says Virat.

Food Price Hike Worsens Poverty in Asia

An annual meeting of asian finance ministers and central bank governors in Hanoi is set to address the fate of 64 million people in the region on the brink of extreme poverty. They are the worst affected by soaring food prices, which have hit record highs in the first two months of this year.

If left unchecked, the food crisis will badly undermine the recent gains in poverty reduction made in Asia. We estimate that a 10 percent rise in domestic food prices in developing Asia could push an additional 64 million people into poverty , based on the 1,25 (dollar) a day poverty line.

New Dangers Arise at Chernobyl

In the aftermath of the anniversary of the worst nuclear disaster in history, Ukrainian authorities have pledged not to abandon those still in need of assistance. But many of the country's policies may be increasing the risk of a new catastrophe.

Chernobyl is still one of the most dangerous nuclear facilities in the world, Arthur Denisnko, energy expert at the National Ecological Centre of Ukraine told IPS.

Human Rights Council Issues First - Ever UN Condemnation of Syria

The special session on syria held by the United Nations Human Rights Council Friday agreed on neither an international mission of enquiry, as originally foreseen, nor a lower level fact - finding mission -only a mission by the office of the High Commisioner for Human Rights.

The attitude taken in the session held at the request of the United States stands in sharp contrast to the strong action taken on Libya just two months ago.
